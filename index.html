Did you know?

In 1914, during World War 1, there was a now famous Christmas truce in the trenches between British and German soldiers. They exchanged gifts across a neutral no man’s land, played football (soccer) together, and decorated their shelves.

Did you know?

The song "Rudolph the Red-Nosed Reindeer" was recorded about a decade after Rudolph himself was created as a character. It was recorded by Gene Autry and over 150 million copies were sold!

Did you know?

The world's largest candy cane was built in 2011 and stands at 63 feet! It was built by a pastry chef from Geneva, Illinois, USA.

Did you know?

In Japan, it is considered tradition to eat Kentucky Fried Chicken (KFC) for Christmas dinner. You have to place an order two months in advance.

Did you know?

The modern Christmas tree originated in 16th century Germany and became popular in mid-19th century England because of Queen Victoria’s husband, Prince Albert (who was German).

Did you know?

Christmas is celebrated on January 7 in countries which are Eastern Orthodox, such as Russia.

Did you know?

Saint Nicholas, the real person Santa Claus was adapted from, lived in 4th century AD in the province of Lycia on the southwest coast of Asia Minor.

Did you know?

Coca-Cola was the first company that used Santa Claus during the winter season for promotion.

Did you know?

America’s first batch of eggnog was made in the Jamestown settlement in 1607. Its name comes from the word “grog”, meaning any drink made with rum. Non-alcoholic eggnog is popular as well.

Did you know?

Christmas stockings were traditionally filled with food to eat, and sometimes small gifts.
